Statistical Physics & The Ising Model

  • © Photo by Josh Calabrese
    • on Unsplash

    Abstract

    In this project, we have studied the Ising model by utilizing one of the most famous and common algorithms used in computational science, the Metropolis algorithm with the aid of Monte Carlo sampling and Markov chains. We have looked into the equilibrium state of a binary system consisting of spins pointing up and down, requiring at least 104 Monte Carlo cycles to reach the most likely state. The phase transition of our system has also been examined, resulting in an estimation of the critical temperature in the thermodynamic limit (TC(L= ∞) ≈ 2.271). Comparing this with the discovery of the critical temperature TC ≈ 2.269 by Lars Onsager, we have observed that our simulations are quite accurate. For simulations of the largest systems over multiple temperatures, it has been highly necessary to include a parallelization in the implementation of the Ising model, due to multiple hour long runtimes.
