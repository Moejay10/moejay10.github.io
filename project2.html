Solving Partial Differential Equations & Eigenvalue Problems with Neural Networks

  • © Photo by Ali Shah Lakhani
    • on Unsplash

    Abstract

    This project is divided into two parts, where the first part gives an analysis of four different methods to approximate a partial differential equation that describes the temperature gradient of a one-dimensional rod with length L=1. The methods implemented are Forward Euler, Backward Euler, Crank-Nicolsen and a deep neural network. It was found that Crank-Nicolsen performed overall best for steps Δx = 0.1 Λ 0.01 with the highest error found being 2.70 × 10-3 and 2.24 × 10-4, respectively. The second part uses a deep neural network to find the eigenvalues of a given symmetric 6 × 6 -matrix, as proposed from the article Computers and Mathematics with Applications 47, 1155 (2004). The eigenvalues converged towards all eigenvalues, and not exclusively the minimum or maximum eigenvalue, as it was proposed from the article.
